The Clan Charter of the Admiralty Board

Ratified in the Spirit of Brotherhood, Seamanship, and Good Cheer

Preamble

Let it be known across every sea, from the shallowest shoal to the deepest blue, that the Captains of this Clan do hereby bind themselves in fellowship. Not by force, nor by fear, but by shared purpose, mutual respect, and the timeless joy of ships under sail.

We are not a navy of conscripts. We are a company of free captains — each choosing to stand beside the others as comrades, rivals, teachers, and friends.

Article I — The Spirit of the Fleet

This Clan exists for one purpose above all:

To enjoy the great game of Naval Action together.

Whether a captain commands a mighty first rate or a humble cutter, all are welcome at our table. Skill is not demanded. Experience is not required. Only good humour, fair conduct, and a willingness to sail with others.

There are no expectations of attendance, performance, or contribution. A captain may sail daily or once a fortnight — all are valued equally.

Article II — Conduct Becoming of a Captain

Every member of this Clan shall:

  • Treat fellow captains with courtesy and respect
  • Offer aid when able, and accept aid when needed
  • Celebrate victories together
  • Endure defeats together
  • Remember that behind every ship is a person seeking enjoyment

We are a fleet, not a factory. We measure success not in ports held, but in good company and good battles.

Article III — Discord & Communication

To strengthen our unity and effectiveness, members are asked to join voice communications as often as possible, nurturing the community spirit and cooperative excellence that define our clan.

Discord is provided for all captains to participate. https://discord.gg/5vNXCZbMfP

This is not a burden, but a necessity — for in the smoke and thunder of battle, silence is defeat.

Article IV — Mutual Support & Shared Prosperity

We are a Clan that helps its own.

Captains shall:

  • Share knowledge freely
  • Assist new sailors in finding their sea legs
  • Provide ships, cannons, or resources when able
  • Escort traders, defend clan waters, and rally to distress calls

No captain shall be left adrift.

Article V — Freedom of the Seas

Every captain retains the right to:

  • Sail any ship they choose
  • Engage in any activity they enjoy
  • Play at their own pace
  • Decline any event without judgement

The Admiralty commands fleets, not lives.

Article VI — Clan Battle Groups

All members to join clan Battle Groups

All hands are expected to muster with the appointed Battle Group whilst they are at sea. This shall not forbid a man from tending to his own ventures; yet be it known that when six or more sail in company, they may be drawn together into action by the fortunes of war. Even so, each captain retains the liberty to pursue his own course whilst remaining in the fleet’s company.

Article VII — Amendments & Evolution

As the winds shift and the seas change, so too may this Charter. Amendments may be proposed by any captain and ratified by consensus.

The Charter is a living document — a compass, not a chain.

Closing Declaration

By this Charter we stand united: a fleet of equals, bound not by obligation, but by camaraderie. May our sails be full, our powder dry, and our spirits high.

Signed,
The Captains of the Admiralty Board
